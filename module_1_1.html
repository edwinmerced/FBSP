Competency 1: Communication Skills

Learning Module 1.1: Effective oral and written communication skills


What is an effective use telephone communication? How do you demonstrate courtesy? What are effective listening skills? How do you provide, follow and interpret oral and written communications? What are e-mail etiquette and ethics?

Learn


Module Introduction. In this module you will explore effective telephone usage. You will also examine courtesy. You will learn how to give, follow and interpret oral and written communications. You will examine effective listening skills. Finally, you will gain knowledge of e-mail etiquette and ethics.
Learning Targets. Upon the completion of this module you will be able to:

  • Demonstrate effective telephone usage and courtesy.
  • Demonstrate effective listening skills.
  • Give, follow and interpret oral and written communications
  • Demonstrate knowledge of e-mail etiquette and ethics

Assess


Module Summary: In this module you explored effective telephone usage. You also examined courtesy. You learned how to give, follow and interpret oral and written communications. You examined effective listening skills. Finally, you gained knowledge of e-mail etiquette and ethics.

Module Exit Assessment 1.1

This assessment will consist of an assignment submission. You will have to demonstrate an understanding of effective telephone etiquette and email communication.

General instructions to access the assessment

To complete this assessment:

  1. Review the module learning materials and perform the practice exercise prior to taking this assessment.
  2. Prepare, review and save a document that will address the two parts of this assignment.
    • Part A: Recall a business telephone conversation that you either heard or participated in. Explain what you think was wrong with the interchange. What would you have done differently?
    • Part B: Prepare an email to a subordinate who is new to the company explaining a new company policy
  3. Press the "Go to Exit Assessment" button.
  4. Follow the instructions to complete your module exit assessment and describe it.
