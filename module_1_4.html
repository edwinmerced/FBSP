Learn


Module Introduction. In this module you will explore orderly, systematic behavior and responsible behavior. You will also examine the concepts of self-understanding, self-esteem, and self-image and their importance to food service managers. Finally you will learn about self-management and stress management techniques.
Learning Targets. Upon the completion of this module you will be able to:

  • Demonstrate orderly, systematic behavior and responsible behavior
  • Explain concepts of self-understanding, self-esteem, and self-image and their importance to food service managers
  • Demonstrate self-management
  • Identify and practice stress management techniques

Assess


Module Summary: In this module you explored orderly, systematic behavior and responsible behavior. You also examined the concepts of self-understanding, self-esteem, and self-image and their importance to food service managers. Finally you learned about self-management and stress management techniques.

Module Exit Assessment 1.4

This assessment will consist of a assignment submission. You will have to demonstrate your understanding of behaviour, self-understanding, self-esteem, and self-image, self-management and stress.

General instructions to access the assessment

To complete this assessment:

  1. Review the module learning materials and perform the practice exercise prior to taking this assessment.
  2. Prepare, review and save a document that will address the two parts of this assignment.
    • Part A: Write a two-page paper describing the leadership behaviors which enabled one of the following corporate leaders to become successful. You can choose: a) Bill Marriott, b) Ray Kroc, c) Steve Jobs, or d. Jack Welch.
    • Part B: Write a two-page paper on why a good self-concept is necessary to be a successful manager/supervisor in the food and beverage industry. Draw upon your experiences interacting with managers, supervisors and peers in the business.
    • Press the "Go to Exit Assessment" button.
    • Follow the instructions to complete your module exit assessment.
